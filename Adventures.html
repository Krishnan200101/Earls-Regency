Cycling

The Earl’s Regency is the ultimate luxury hotel to stay at in Kandy. Kandy has a cool mountain climate 600m above sea level. Get away from the tropical heat of the lowlands and enter the misty mountains. One of the best places for cycling would be "The Hanthana Mountain Range" which lies in central Sri Lanka, south-west of the city of Kandy. This experience is mostly a downhill back road cycling trip (28kms) winding through tea estates and smaller towns in the back roads of Kandy. We will meet at the top of the Hanthana Hill and ride down through winding back roads to Peradeniya University. Along the way enjoy views of the surrounding valleys, tea estates and small-town village life. Visit a Hindu Temple. As well as other small surprises along the route.

Hiking

Although there are many activities focused on the city’s rich history, the more adventurous visitors can also take on a hike up the Alagalla Mountain Range or the Knuckles Mountain Range, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Knuckles mountain range is a series of peaks which, from a distance, resembles the knuckles of a closed fist. It was named a UNESCO World Heritage site and is wonderful for trekking and hiking, giving climbers a unique insight to the flora and fauna available there. Another nearby mountain that climbers would enjoy is the Hanthana Mountain Range

Perahera and Sri Dalada Maligawa

There are many places to see and things to do in Kandy which visitors cannot experience in any other part of the island. Amongst the most popular activities in Kandy would be to pay a visit to the sacred Sri Dalada Maligawa that gives visitors an inside look into the city’s history and tradition as it houses the relic of the tooth of Lord Buddha. If you happen to visit in July or August you can witness the Esala Perahera, an annual procession in which the tooth relic of Lord Buddha is carried through the streets accompanied by dancers and drummers. Nearby visitors can find the last royal palace built in Sri Lanka, which is in the temple complex, and now houses the National Museum of Kandy. The “centre” of Kandy, the main focal point in which the rest of the city seems to revolve around, is none other than Kandy Lake and is recognizable by the walakulu, or ‘clouds wall’ surrounding it.
